Street Photography

CANDID SCENE

Reflection Questions

1. What story does your photo tell?
The story of the photo tells how, even as the school year ends for elementary and high school students, college students continue walking with purpose along the quiet street. The stores begin to open, ready to support those still focused on their studies and daily routines. Under the soft morning light, their journey reflects determination and responsibility. While others take a break, they keep moving forward, embracing the demands of college life.

2. Why did you choose this subject?
I chose this subject as a symbol of a college student’s life, showing both its meaning and everyday reality. The street reflects countless moments where students laugh, cry, and reconnect with former classmates. It also captures simple routines, like eating at nearby siomai stores while taking a break from their responsibilities. In essence, the scene represents the shared experiences and memories formed in this stage of life.

3. What elements make your photo effective?
The photo is effective because of the filter from the Fuji Cam app, which enhances the colors and gives it a warm, nostalgic tone. The color of the sky blends well with the surroundings, creating a balanced and natural look. The composition is also strong, with the street leading the viewer’s eye toward the students and tricycle, adding depth to the image. Additionally, the everyday scene captures a realistic and relatable moment, making the photo more engaging and meaningful.

4. What challenges did you encounter?
The challenges I encountered were capturing the right moment because the students were constantly walking and moving. The motorcycle also passed by quickly, making it difficult to get a clear and well-timed shot. It was hard to balance the timing and composition while everything in the scene was in motion. Moreover, I had to be patient and observant to capture a meaningful moment from the busy street.

5. How can you improve your shot next time?
Next time, I can improve my shot by choosing a better timing to capture clearer and more focused subjects. I could also adjust the angle or position to create a stronger composition and reduce distractions in the background. Improving the lighting or exposure settings would help enhance the details and overall quality of the photo. Lastly, I can be more patient and anticipate the movement in the scene to capture a more impactful moment.
